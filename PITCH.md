# Pitch

## 90-second pitch

AI is becoming one of humanity's most important tools, but the infrastructure behind it is increasingly concentrated among organizations able to finance enormous data centers.

At the same time, billions of personal devices contain useful capacity that often sits idle. People can consume AI, but they have almost no way to participate in its infrastructure.

GridOne AI changes that.

**Don't just use AI. Help build it.**

GridOne is a Collaborative Intelligence Network. People become Builders by choosing when and how their devices contribute. Smart Contribution protects foreground use, power, and temperature limits. The network assigns only bounded, allow-listed AI microtasks—never arbitrary remote code.

In our Build Week prototype, a real job is divided across multiple worker processes. If a Builder pauses, the work is safely reassigned. Results are verified through task-specific checks and selective redundancy. Only verified work earns prototype AI Credits, and every credit links to a transparent task receipt.

The long-term vision is a cooperative layer for workloads that benefit from distributed capacity. Developers gain another way to run suitable AI tasks. Builders earn useful AI access. AI providers build a closer relationship with the communities they serve.

GridOne is not replacing data centers, and our prototype credits are not yet redeemable. We are proving the smallest honest loop: community compute, verified work, and shared value.

**One grid. Millions of Builders. A shared AI future.**

## Judge Q&A

### Is this just another decentralized compute marketplace?

GridOne's differentiator is the closed value loop: safe AI-specific tasks, verification before reward, transparent receipts, and credits intended to unlock AI access rather than a speculative token. The cooperative participation model is part of the product, not only the supply mechanism.

### Can this train frontier models?

Not today, and that is not the MVP claim. Frontier training requires tightly coupled accelerators and high-speed interconnects. GridOne begins with divisible, latency-tolerant, independently verifiable workloads.

### How is privacy protected?

The prototype uses public or synthetic inputs, minimizes payloads, runs only allow-listed tasks, and stores little data. Splitting and transport encryption help but are not sufficient for arbitrary sensitive workloads; those remain out of scope.

### How do you stop workers from cheating?

Credits are gated by task-specific validation, known test vectors, and redundant sampling. Stronger reputation, attestation, and cryptographic methods are future work.

### Are the credits usable in ChatGPT or Codex?

No. Build Week credits are simulated and non-redeemable. Those services illustrate a future reward category that would require provider agreements and sustainable economics.

### Why would a Builder participate?

Useful rewards matter, but so do purpose, transparency, and belonging. GridOne makes contribution visible and controlled, allowing someone to say, “I helped build this.” The team will validate whether that motivation persists beyond early enthusiasts.

### Why would an AI provider participate?

For suitable workloads, GridOne could add flexible capacity and a community participation channel. It complements rather than replaces efficient centralized infrastructure.
