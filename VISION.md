# Vision

## North star

**Turn humanity into the world's largest collaborative AI infrastructure.**

GridOne AI enables anyone, anywhere, to participate in building the future of artificial intelligence by securely contributing idle device capacity.

## The problem

AI demand is increasing the importance and cost of compute infrastructure. Access is concentrated among organizations that can finance specialized hardware, energy, networking, and data centers.

At the same time, billions of computers, gaming PCs, laptops, and emerging AI-capable devices contain unused capacity. The exact amount of economically usable idle compute is an open research question; the opportunity must be measured rather than assumed.

The human gap is clearer: ordinary people can use AI, but have few meaningful ways to participate in its infrastructure and share in the resulting access.

## The category

GridOne is a **Collaborative Intelligence Network** and an **AI Compute Cooperative**.

It is not positioned as a replacement for hyperscale data centers. It is a complementary layer for workloads that tolerate geographic distribution, variable availability, higher latency, and redundant verification.

Likely early workloads include evaluation, embeddings, document transformation, synthetic-data filtering, batch inference, rendering, simulations, and other bounded tasks that can be safely packaged and independently checked.

## The flywheel

```text
More Builders
    ↓
More verified capacity
    ↓
More suitable AI workloads
    ↓
More useful AI Credits
    ↓
More accessible AI tools
    ↓
More Builders
```

## Who benefits

- **Builders** gain purpose, visibility, control, and AI Credits.
- **Developers** gain an additional compute option and a relationship with the community using their tools.
- **AI providers** gain a community-powered layer for suitable workloads and a tangible participation model.
- **Researchers, universities, and public-interest teams** gain potential access to capacity that would otherwise be difficult to finance.
- **Society** gains an experiment in distributing participation and benefits more broadly.

## Strategic posture

GridOne does not assume compute is free. Builders pay for electricity, device wear, connectivity, and opportunity cost. The network must make those costs visible and create benefits that outweigh them.

GridOne does not assume credits can initially buy third-party services such as ChatGPT or Codex. The MVP credits are simulated network accounting. Real redemption requires commercial agreements, sustainable unit economics, and clear terms.

GridOne does not assume every AI workload belongs on untrusted consumer hardware. Scheduling begins with safe, divisible, verifiable workloads and expands only with evidence.

## Long-term success

GridOne succeeds when:

- Builders can contribute without noticeable interruption or hidden costs.
- Workload owners can predict cost, latency, reliability, and privacy.
- Credits represent transparent, useful, and sustainable value.
- Verification makes fraud uneconomic for supported task classes.
- The network complements efficient data centers instead of making inflated replacement claims.
- People genuinely feel: **“I helped build this.”**

## Five-year horizon

If the model works, GridOne becomes an open coordination layer across personal computers, workstations, edge servers, and compatible mobile devices. Developers submit supported AI tasks through one API; the scheduler selects eligible Builders; verified contributions are rewarded; and cooperative governance gives the community a voice in how the network evolves.
